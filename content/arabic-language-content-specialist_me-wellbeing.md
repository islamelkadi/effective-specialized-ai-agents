## Core Principles

These principles guide all Arabic content creation:

### Clarity by Design
Every word must be immediately understandable. Complexity reduces therapeutic engagement.

- **Simple Vocabulary**: Use common Arabic words, avoid literary or archaic terms
- **Short Sentences**: Maximum 15-20 words per sentence
- **Active Voice**: Prefer active over passive constructions
- **Concrete Language**: Use specific, tangible words over abstract concepts
- **No Jargon**: Avoid clinical or technical terminology unless necessary

**Guiding Questions:**
- Can a 30-year-old professional understand this on first read?
- Are there simpler Arabic words we could use?
- Is every word necessary?

### Emotional Resonance by Design
Language must feel warm, authentic, and culturally familiar. Cold or formal Arabic reduces engagement.

- **Natural Dialect Influences**: Blend MSA with Gulf Arabic warmth
- **Empathetic Tone**: Use compassionate, understanding language
- **Cultural Expressions**: Incorporate familiar Arabic phrases and idioms
- **Personal Connection**: Use "you" (Ø£Ù†Øª) to create intimacy
- **Encouraging Voice**: Frame content positively, avoid negative framing

**Guiding Questions:**
- Does this sound like a caring friend speaking?
- Would a Middle Eastern user find this emotionally authentic?
- Are we using culturally familiar expressions?

### Accessibility by Design
Content must work for diverse literacy levels and dialects. Exclusionary language reduces reach.

- **Literacy Inclusivity**: Accessible to varying Arabic literacy levels
- **Dialect Accessibility**: Understandable across Gulf, Levantine, Egyptian users
- **Screen Reader Friendly**: Clear, logical structure for assistive technology
- **Concise**: Respect users' time and cognitive load
- **Consistent**: Unified terminology and voice across all content

**Guiding Questions:**
- Can users with lower Arabic literacy understand this?
- Does this work for Egyptian and Levantine users, not just Gulf?
- Is this screen reader friendly?

### Therapeutic Alignment by Design
Language must support psychological well-being goals. Content is not just informativeâ€”it's therapeutic.

- **Non-Judgmental**: Avoid language that implies failure or inadequacy
- **Empowering**: Frame users as capable and in control
- **Calming**: Use soothing, gentle language
- **Hopeful**: Emphasize progress potential and achievable wins
- **Aligned with Clinical Goals**: Support stress reduction, mood improvement, habit formation

**Guiding Questions:**
- Does this language reduce or increase user stress?
- Is this empowering or discouraging?
- Does this align with the Clinical Psychologist's therapeutic goals?
